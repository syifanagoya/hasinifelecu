Is Betty Broderick Still in Prison?

Betty Broderick has been in prison for 30 years after being found guilty of murdering her ex-husband and his new bride in their bedroom. She is unlikely to be released anytime in the near future. In 1991, Betty was convicted to 32 years to life in prison for the murders of her ex-husband Dan Broderick, 44, a well-known San Diego lawyer, and his wife, Linda Kolkena Broderick, 28.

Did Betty Broderick get Parole?

Betty is still behind bars, charged with second-degree murder. In 1991, she was sentenced to 32 years to life in prison, including two 15-year terms for the murders. She is currently incarcerated at the California Institution for Women, where she has been rejected parole twice: once in 2010 and again in 2017. The court refused her parole for the maximum period (15 years), which means she would be eligible for re-eligibility in 2032. She might be allowed sooner if she meets specific criteria, such as good behaviour. The parole board in California unanimously decided to oppose her release. Therefore, she'll be 84 years old when she's up for parole again in 2032.
