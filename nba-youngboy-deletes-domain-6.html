NBA YoungBoy Deletes Twitter After Dissing Drake &amp; Lil Yachty

NBA YoungBoy has reportedly deleted his Twitter account after dissing Drake and Lil Yachty on a recent track.

On Friday (May 12), YoungBoy took to Twitter to declare that he’s leaving the popular social media app for good.

“Enough said !!!!!! Twitter deactivated.” the Baton Rouge native Tweeted from his @baeheltop handle, which is no longer active.

We aren’t sure if this is a permanent fixture or temporary break, but regardless, NBA YoungBoy will still be in the rap conversation.

NBA YoungBoy has been on a recent rampage against some of the music industry’s biggest rap stars.

On May 12, NBA YoungBoy taunted Lil Durk after the Chicago rapper avoided a showdown by pushing his Almost Healed album back at the last minute.

YoungBoy had plenty of smoke for Durkio with the arrival of his new project Richest Opp on Friday (May 12), and trolled the OTF honcho in a Twitter video while flexing stacks of cash.

“Bitch ass n-gga, you pushed back. You bet not ever try it again. Don’t play with me. Flood my watch, mama, this Lil Top, n-gga,” the Baton Rouge native said in the clip while dancing to “I Got That Shit,” one of 17 new tracks off his aforementioned project.

YoungBoy kept his foot on Durk’s next by replying to a tweet from the Chicago native promoting his new single “All My Life” with J. Cole, which also arrived on Friday.

“It’s been up you a bitch n-gga stay down ‘You bet not drop,’” he write, before adding: “Bitch you ain’t never gone troll a gangster again.”

As for NBA YoungBoy’s Richest Opp, the 17-track project contains a particularly vicious track called “Fuck the Industry Pt. 2,” which is loaded with lyrical darts aimed at Drake, J. Cole and Lil Yachty.

“Talk to Drake ‘cross FaceTime, he wasn’t feelin’ me/ Told me that he fuck with Durk, damn, that shit was gettin’ to me/ Told me that he like the shit I’m doin’, but can’t do shit with me/ So when we cross our ways, fuck what you say, bitch, you my enemy,” the Baton Rouge rapper spits.

He then calls Cole a “ho” for apparently ghosting him after they made plans to work together, rapping: “J a ho, that n-gga played it cold, like he was gon’ do a feature/ So I texted his line, a muscle sign, I swear it’s gon’ be nice to meet you.”

NBA YoungBoy caps off the venomous track by calling out Lil Yachty over his prior relationship with City Girls’ JT.

“Bitch, play with me, Lil Yachty mad about JT, f-ggot/ Bitch, play with me, half of y’all ain’t got more money than me,” he spits.

NBA YoungBoy’s Richest Opp was released via Never Broke Again/Motown Records and includes 17 new tracks, with a sole feature from NBA Ben10. It’s his third release of the year following January’s I Rest My Case and April’s Don’t Try This at Home.
