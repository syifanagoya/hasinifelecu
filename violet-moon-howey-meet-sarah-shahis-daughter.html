Violet Moon Howey’s Parents Divorced after 10 Years of Marriage

Violet’s parents got divorced in 2021 after 10 years of their marriage. Steve Howey and Sarah Shahi’s love story was love at first sight as it started after their first meet-up on set in the sitcom Reba in 2004. After which, the duo got talking and got to know each other. As time went on, their platonic relationship snowballed into a romantic relationship, which led to the two love birds tying the knot on the 7th of March 2009.

Unfortunately, their love story didn’t have a happy ending as things went sour between the both of them after 10 years of marriage, which eventually led to a divorce. In an Interview, Steve Howey stated that the reason for their divorce was that it was no longer easy to keep up with their hectic work schedule as he was engaged in filming the final season of the TV series “Shameless” at the same time, Sarah was also engrossed with several filming projects.

Sarah’s Career and the Demands of Motherhood Contributed to Her Divorce with Steve Howey

The pressure of motherhood, coupled with keeping up with her career and other family responsibilities, made her lose the feelings she once had towards her then-husband to the point they lived more like strangers even though they were a couple. This led to their filing for divorce in May 2020, which was then finalized in 2021. The grounds for their divorce were irreconcilable differences. 

Violet Moon Howey currently lives with her mother, Sarah Shahi, in Los Angeles, California. However, Steve Howey is still part of her life as both parents decided to co-parent their kids, and they are doing a nice job in it.

She also wanted to disprove the perception that women are incapable of natural delivery and that they would always need the assistance of doctors, epidurals, and doctors by going through home birth herself. This led to Violet and her twin brother being birthed at home with the assistance of a professional midwife.

Her decision came with consequences, as there were some complications in the course of their delivery.
